Who I Am

About Me

I'm a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical Engineering at the University of Rhode Island, working in the Translational Neurorobotics Lab under Dr. Reza Abiri.

My research focuses on real-time robotic systems that respond to human biosignals — integrating EEG/EMG acquisition, force sensing, and embedded hardware into closed-loop platforms for upper-limb motor rehabilitation.

I'm broadly interested in the translation of neuroscience-informed signal processing into usable assistive technology — making rehabilitation robotics smarter, more adaptive, and more human.

What I've Done

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant (Ph.D.) Current · Jan. 2023 – Present
Translational Neurorobotics Lab, University of Rhode Island · Kingston, RI
  • Enhanced stability and precision of a magnetically actuated upper-limb rehabilitation platform by implementing a closed-loop Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) for real-time, sensorless state estimation and control
  • Developed a Python interface for real-time visualization and trajectory control of the platform via Arduino-based feedback
  • Advanced motor intention decoding by constructing brain-signal feature-extraction pipelines based on bispectrum, cross-bispectrum, and phase-coupling metrics
  • Designed a real-time closed-loop robotics software framework integrating EEG/EMG acquisition, force/torque sensing, and a 4-DOF linkage robot for end-to-end data collection and control
  • Implemented a biosignal-driven assistive control algorithm that decodes human intent from EEG/EMG to command the robot in real time
  • Engineered a physics-based PyBullet simulation of a 4-DOF rehabilitation robot interfaced with a custom load-cell joystick, enabling interactive force-controlled human-in-the-loop testing
  • Developed an adaptive admittance control framework for the 4-DOF rehabilitation robot
  • Contributed to signal-processing pipelines integrating wavelet, ICA, and feature extraction for comparative analysis of EEG and tEEG signal performance in movement decoding
  • Collaborated on a self-training Deep Q-Network (DQN) framework that enhances biosignal-driven robotic control accuracy under noisy input conditions
Graduate Research Intern 2021 – 2022
School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Tehran · Tehran, Iran
  • Explored advanced biosignal-processing techniques and their integration with ML/DL algorithms to enhance robotic control and human-robot interaction
Graduate Research Assistant (M.S.) 2019 – 2021
Department of Biomedical Engineering and Medical Physics, SBMU · Tehran, Iran
  • Processed and analyzed fMRI/MRI data with deep learning architectures (CNN, LSTM, CNN-LSTM) for early Alzheimer's detection
  • Developed Arduino-based systems integrating ECG signals, Bluetooth, and ESP8266 modules for biosignal acquisition, cloud transmission, and ML classification
